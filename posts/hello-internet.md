I build infrastructure at Google. I've helped systems scale to millions of users. I've saved thousands of dev hours with LLMs.

And yet, somehow, building a personal website was always *"something I'll get to later."*

Later is now.

---

## Why now?

Partly because I kept telling junior engineers to "build in public" and document their work, while having absolutely nothing to show for myself online. Peak hypocrisy.

Partly because I've been collecting thoughts — about distributed systems, about LLMs in production, about what it really takes to scale software — and they deserve somewhere to live besides my notes app and 3am voice memos.

And partly because a personal site is one of those rare things in engineering that you actually *own*. No quarterly priorities, no design reviews, no approval chains. Just me, a text editor, and the freedom to write whatever I want.

## What to expect here

I'll write about things I find genuinely interesting:

- **Distributed systems** — consistency models, failure modes, and why CAP theorem is both correct and slightly misleading
- **LLMs in production** — not the hype, but the actual unglamorous work of making them reliable
- **Database internals** — I read storage engine source code for fun, which is either admirable or concerning
- **Career stuff** — going from a college kid who built a Mars rover to SWE-III at Google

I'll also occasionally write something ridiculous. That's a promise.
